Fact #4

Myth of the Nuns @ Rose Garden

The “Three Sisters” statues at CSU Chico, often called the “nuns,” were created in 1991 by artist Deborah Masters with help from students. Inspired by Anton Chekhov's play Three Sisters, the sculptures represent deep emotional states—appearing sad and thoughtful from the front but strong from the back. Though many myths have developed around them, such as hidden cameras in their eyes or haunted behavior at night, these are just fun campus legends with no truth behind them.

  • Some students believe the statues are haunted or have cameras in their eyes—but neither is true.

  • They're located near the Rose Garden and are one of the most talked-about art pieces on campus.

    Fact #5

    Bike Life

    Chico earned its reputation as a top-tier bike town—once ranked America's #1 cycling city by Bicycle Magazine in 1997—and hosts the exhilarating Wildflower Century, an annual springtime festival and fundraiser organized by Chico Velo. Each late-April weekend, thousands of riders choose from routes ranging from a 12-mile family-friendly spin to grueling 100- or 125-mile century and "megaflower" challenges, all starting and ending at the Silver Dollar Fairgrounds with fully supported rest stops, SAG vehicles, catered meals, a lively WildFest expo, and local beer on tap

  • Varied route options — 8 distinct courses (Childflower, Mildflower, Metricflower, Flatflower, Valleyflower, Wildflower 95/100, Mountainflower, Megaflower 125) cater to all abilities, with elevation gains up to ~7,600' on the longest

  • Strong community vibe — the pre-ride WildFest features food trucks, bike skills demos, vendor tents, and a beer garden, while all ride proceeds fuel Chico Velo's bike advocacy and local cycling projects
