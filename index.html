Myth of the Nuns @ Rose Garden

The Statue

The “Three Sisters” statues at CSU Chico, often called the “nuns,” were created in 1991 by artist Deborah Masters with help from students. Inspired by Anton Chekhov's play Three Sisters, the sculptures represent deep emotional states—appearing sad and thoughtful from the front but strong from the back. Though many myths have developed around them, such as hidden cameras in their eyes or haunted behavior at night, these are just fun campus legends with no truth behind them.

  • Some students believe the statues are haunted or have cameras in their eyes—but neither is true.

  • They're located near the Rose Garden and are one of the most talked-about art pieces on campus.
